Analysis

Pros:

  • The variety and versatility of extensions mean that anyone could find one that’s useful to them
  • Browser devs have taken steps to improve security
  • Google actively takes down extensions that have been reported and found to be malicious, and does not allow the installation of extensions from outside their store

Cons:

  • Extensions can be malicious as they have access to your full browser history, can log keystrokes, selling your data, or redirecting your browser
  • Extensions can be updated without the user’s consent or knowledge, meaning that a once good extension could be updated to be malicious without the user knowing
  • There are instances of extensions being sold, and the new owners turn them into malicious extensions

Team's Word on Browser Extensions

Browser Extensions can be useful and safe when they come from reliable and dependable sources. Not all web users use extensions and it only makes sense to use extensions if what they accomplish is useful to the user and in the best interest of the user. The users must consider how much they are ready to disclose or give access when installing extensions on their browser. Although modern browsers such as Google Chrome or Firefox have taken steps to protect its users and detect unreliable sources, it is still the responsibility of the users to know what they are downloading on their browser so personal data stay secure.

Future Potential/Directions

The capabilities of browser extensions are dependent on the browser being used. When browser extensions were first being used they could access a lot of the internal workings of the browsers. As browsers have become more secure it has limited the internal workings that extensions can access and thus reduced the abilities of extensions [2]. With the development of the WebExtensions Community Group in 2021 we should see browser extensions becoming even more unified across platforms, the eventual shared standard developed by the WebExtensions Community Group making it easier to bridge the differences between browsers. A shared standard will reduce difficulties for developers by specifying consistent model and common core functionality and hopefully having one browser extension for all major browsers will increase performance and make browser extensions more secure and resistant to abuse [11].
